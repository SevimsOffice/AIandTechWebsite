Most people who use Claude daily — even power users — are operating at 20–30% of what the full ecosystem can do. Not because they're not smart. Because no one has mapped the whole stack for them.

This prompt changes that. Paste it into Claude and get a 5,000+ word, operator-grade strategic report that diagnoses your current usage, maps every surface you're underusing, and gives you a week-by-week implementation roadmap.

The prompt is on the next two pages. Here's how to get the most out of it.

Step 1 — Fill In Your Context

Find the two placeholders in the prompt and replace them with your real information. The more specific you are, the more accurate and actionable your report will be.

Placeholder What to Write Example
[Agency / Business] Your business type and what you primarily use Claude for Boutique HR consulting firm. I use Claude for compensation benchmarking, interview design, org strategy, and client reports.
[Agency / Business] (second use) Same — Claude will use this to personalize section-level recommendations Same as above, or be more specific: "10-person fractional CMO agency serving Series A–C startups."

Step 2 — Paste Into Claude

Open a new chat in Claude (claude.ai) and paste the entire prompt at once. Do not break it into pieces — the report structure depends on the full prompt being in a single message.

Tip: Use Claude Sonnet or Opus for this. Haiku will produce a noticeably shorter and less detailed report. For maximum depth, use Opus with Extended Thinking enabled if you have access.

Step 3 — Read, Highlight, Implement

Your report will be 5,000+ words. Read it in full once. Then:

Highlight

Mark every gap that feels uncomfortably accurate. Those are your highest-leverage starting points.

Save It

Save the report as a Claude Project document. Reference it during your weekly review.

Week 1 Only

Don't try to implement everything. Pick the single highest-ROI use case from Section 4 and start there.

Re-Run Monthly

Your Claude usage will evolve. Re-run this prompt once a month to get an updated diagnosis.
